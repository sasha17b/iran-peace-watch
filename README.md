## Bottom line

> **First hard instruments appear — but every one is temporary.** Two things turned this week that are *not* Iran's one-sided claims. (1) The US Treasury issued a documented legal instrument: **OFAC General License X (23 Jun)** authorising purchase and resale of Iranian crude and petroleum — confirmed publicly by Treasury Secretary Bessent. (2) **IAEA chief Grossi (24 Jun)** stated the MOU *explicitly* places Iran's nuclear material and facilities under IAEA supervision, pushing back on both Washington's and Tehran's spin. Real, verifiable, US-/international-side progress — the first since the memorandum. **But the catch is durability:** GL X is a temporary waiver that *expires 21 Aug*, Hormuz transit is toll-free "60 days only" before Iran resumes control and charges, Iran still bars inspectors at the bombed sites, Pezeshkian (25 Jun) again refuses to concede the right to enrich, and the UNSC-irreversibility track is still at zero. The variable our target LPs (Partners Group, LGT Capital Partners) will underwrite — *legally durable, non-reversible sanctions relief* — has **not** triggered. A temporary waiver is not a termination schedule.
>
> **So what for OUDRA:** advance the fund build on the critical path now, but **hold formal LP outreach** until the relief is verified, scheduled, and signalled irreversible. A 60-day waiver that lapses 21 Aug is not yet underwritable.
>

> **Comfort score: 🟠 ~35 / 100 — not ready (early preparation).** Process keeps moving — a documented US sanctions instrument (OFAC GL X) and IAEA-supervision language now exist on paper — but every instrument so far is temporary and reversible, so the market preconditions that make a Partners Group / LGT CP conversation credible remain unmet. Threshold to open first *informal* talks: **~75 / 100**; to open a formal raise: ~90.

**Score bands:** 0–25 not ready · 26–50 early preparation · 51–75 approaching · 76–90 ready for first talks · 91–100 ready to raise.

**Binding constraint:** of the two deal-breakers, the sanctions-relief pathway has now produced a real US instrument — **OFAC GL X (23 Jun)**, publicly confirmed by Treasury — which lifts it to ◑ 50. But the instrument is a *temporary* waiver expiring 21 Aug, not a termination *schedule*, and signalled legal irreversibility (the UNSC track) is still at zero. The score can drift up through early preparation but cannot approach the talks threshold until the relief is made durable and scheduled **and** the irreversibility track turns.
