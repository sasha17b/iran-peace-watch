## Bottom line

> **Durability was tested this weekend — and failed.** The variable our target LPs (Partners Group, LGT Capital Partners) would underwrite is *legally durable, non-reversible* relief. This week the ceasefire itself — the precondition beneath everything — broke in the open. **(1)** On **27 Jun** the US struck five Iranian targets (surveillance, communications, air defence, drone storage and minelayer capabilities). **(2)** On **28 Jun** the IRGC retaliated with ballistic missiles and drones against the **US Ali Al Salem airbase in Kuwait** and the **US Fifth Fleet at Port Salman, Bahrain** — Bahrain reported a damaged residential building near its airport (no deaths), Kuwait intercepted; Bahrain called it a sovereignty violation and Iran threatened to halt talks. **(3)** The **Strait of Hormuz collapsed again** — transit fell from ~70 vessels (25 Jun) to ~12 (28 Jun). By **29 Jun** both sides stood down "for now," vessels resumed moving (22 crossings) and oil settled near $69, but the episode proved in real time that the 60-day window is reversible at will. The paper gains survive — **OFAC General License X** is still in force and Pezeshkian announced **$6bn of $12bn in Qatar-held frozen assets** for release — but the thing allocators actually price, *that the war stays over,* was just disproven.
>
> **So what for OUDRA:** keep building the fund on the critical path, but **formal LP outreach stays on hold** — and the bar moves up, not down. A relief package layered on a ceasefire that can break over a weekend is not underwritable. We now need to see durability demonstrated *over time*, not merely asserted.
>

## Readiness for first LP talks — conditions & comfort score

> **Comfort score: 🔴 ~25 / 100 — not ready (slipped from early preparation).** The legal instruments from last week did not evaporate — GL X stands and a $6bn asset tranche is now moving — but a documented ceasefire breach, cross-border strikes on Gulf states, and a second Hormuz closure reset the most basic market precondition. An allocator cannot price a deployment thesis the week the underlying war reignites. Threshold to open first *informal* talks: **~75 / 100**; to open a formal raise: ~90.

What must hold true before a credible first conversation. Progress scale: ○ 0 · ◔ 25 · ◑ 50 · ◕ 75 · ● 100

| Condition | Why PG / LGT CP need it | Progress |
|---|---|:--:|
| Ceasefire durable, no resumption of war | No allocator underwrites an active conflict | ◔ 25 |
| Final framework deal signed and holding | Confirms a real political path exists | ◑ 50 |
| Credible, scheduled sanctions-relief pathway | The legal gate to deploying capital | ◑ 50 |
| Signalled legal irreversibility (UNSC track) | Protection against snap-back risk | ○ 0 |
| Emerging capital convertibility / repatriation | LPs must be able to get money out | ◔ 25 |

**Score bands:** 0–25 not ready · 26–50 early preparation · 51–75 approaching · 76–90 ready for first talks · 91–100 ready to raise.

**Binding constraint:** the sanctions-relief pathway is the *less* binding of the two deal-breakers right now — a real US instrument (GL X) exists and money is starting to move. The binding constraint has reverted to **the ceasefire itself**: durability dropped from ◑ 50 to ◔ 25 after the 27–28 Jun strikes and counter-strikes, and signalled legal irreversibility (the UNSC track) is still at zero. The score cannot re-approach the talks threshold until the ceasefire is demonstrably held *over weeks*, the relief is made durable and scheduled, **and** the irreversibility track turns.
